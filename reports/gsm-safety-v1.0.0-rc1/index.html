ECG data noteThe QTc inflation reported at safety.viz#79 is cleaned. The CDISC Pilot 01 source collects RR and heart rate as separate measurements and the two contradict each other — they were generated independently (corr(RR, 60000/HR) = 0.0095; only 68 of 8,220 paired readings agree within 5%). Its pre-derived QTc parameters are corrected against that collected RR, which is where the inflation came from. This is dirty source data, not an upstream defect: admiral’s ADEG template sets rr_code = "RR" deliberately and pharmaverseadam runs it faithfully — both correctly propagate an inconsistency already in the source. The build now corrects against the RR implied by the recorded heart rate, the more credible of the two inputs, with a guard that fails if RR contradicts HR again. Median QTcF drops 555 → 468 ms and the categorical view now discriminates between arms (>450 ms: Placebo 84.5%, Xanomeline High 91.7%, Xanomeline Low 79.8%) instead of pinning every arm near 100%. What remains is the source rather than the chart: the pilot’s measured QT is long (median 444 ms), so a majority still cross the ICH E14 categories. The demo exercises every view and threshold, but its crossing rates are not clinically typical.
