obot

Jeremy Wildfire's open-source safety-graphics modernization portfolio — planned, tracked, and largely built with AI agents in the loop.

The portfolio modernizes the SafetyGraphics interactive clinical-safety displays — nine archived renderers built on decade-old JavaScript — into a single maintained charting library with requirement-traceable tests, consumed from R the same way gsm.kri consumes gsm.viz. Work is captured as Requirement issues in obot.roadmap and implemented in the repos below.

How work happens

Requirements move through a six-stage lifecycle (Backlog → Requirement Gathering → Design → Development → Review → Release) tracked on the obot Roadmap project. Design documents live under requirements/design/; agents draft, humans sign off. Conventions follow gsm.roadmap and gsm.agent.

History

From May to June 2026 this portfolio ran as "Open Source OrangeBot" on the obot-claw hub — an autonomous-agent experiment with nightly briefings, whose story is told in the framework report. The hub was archived in July 2026; its diary and reports were migrated here, and this site replaced it as the project's memory.

Maintained in jwildfire/obot.roadmap · built with AI assistance · metrics and roadmap regenerate daily.
